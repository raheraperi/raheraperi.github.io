Cultural Blog - Time + Habits

March 2018

Which timebox and habits techniques did you try?

I found the Pomodoro technique simple and straightforward to maintain focus on the task at hand.
Mindfulness, meditation and yoga have been a huge part of my life for eight years. I find that my optimal flow comes when I have a regular yoga and meditation practice. You're less caught up in the stories the mind tells us, particularly those that try and tell us we're not good enough or worthy.

What did you find interesting?

I found Carol Dweck's concepts of the fixed vs. growth mindsets to be very insightful.
A fixed mindset is a bi-product of the conventional, old school pedagogy of our current education system. Understanding that our talents and abilities can develop through effort rather than being inate/fixed is vital. If you believe you can improve and put in the effort, then you will see results!

What worked for you?

It probably sounds cheesy, but I tried out some new positivity mantras to counteract any negative thoughts. I found these to be super effective when I caught my imposter syndrome symptoms creeping up.

Which will you experiment further with?

I will be experimenting more with tiny habits. I used to be a terrible procrastinator, and although this has somewhat improved I think there are lots of efficiencies to be discovered in my work/study life through creating tiny habits.
Changing behaviour is no small feat, but the Fogg method is all about simplicity!
