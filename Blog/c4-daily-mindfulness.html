Cultural Blog - Daily Mindfulness

10 April 2018

Mindfulness Conversation

Learnings:

  • Listening - I'm a bit of an introvert at times which I think intrinsically makes me a good listener. I found this bit really easy.
  • Dipping - As humans we attach a lot of meaning onto the things people say to us. I have a habit of drawing conclusions or making assumptions and reading into the things people (especially friends and family) say to me. It's easy to misinterpret these messages so I found it extremely helpful to take a mindful moment to check in with myself to ensure I wasn't making meaning out of nothing.
  • Looping - I reckon looping could have saved a lot of my past failed relationships! Feeding back to your loved one to confirm what you heard aligned with what they intended is crucial to avoid any misunderstandings.

I subtly tested this on a love interest and I think it made him feel heard and understood.

Sustaining my Practice

Take one breath a day

I chose this because of its simplicity. Sometimes we get lost in the mind's chatter and we need to check in. When we become aware of the mind's distractions, we can take one breath, similar to a reset button. During a busy day we can give ourself the gift of a mindful breath as an act of kindness towards ourselves.
